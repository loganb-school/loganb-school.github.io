Some of the structures in the respretory system are the

  • Lungs
  • Nose
  • Mouth
  • Pharynx
  • Larynx
  • Trachea
  • Bronchi/Bronchioles
  • Alveoli
  • Diaphragm

The Lungs

Pair of spongy, pinkish-gray organs in your chest.

The Nose

Structure between the eyes that serves as the entrance to the respiratory tract and contains the olfactory organ.

The Mouth

Oval-shaped cavity inside the skull. The two main functions of the mouth are eating and speaking.

The Pharynx

Hollow tube that starts behind the nose, goes down the neck, and ends at the top of the trachea and esophagus.

The Larynx

Hollow tube that lets air pass from your throat (pharynx) to your trachea on the way to your lungs. It also contains your vocal cords and is essential to human speech.

The Trachea

A large membranous tube reinforced by rings of cartilage, extending from the larynx to the bronchial tubes and conveying air to and from the lungs; the windpipe.

The Bronchi/Bronchioles

Any of the major air passages of the lungs which diverge from the windpipe.

The Alveoli

Any of the many tiny air sacs of the lungs which allow for gaseous exchange.

The Diaphragm

Major muscle of respiration. It is a large, dome-shaped muscle that contracts rhythmically and continually.
