Ted Talk Notes

36% Of life is spent sleeping, (if someone lives to 90, 32 years would have been sleeping). Shakespeare wrote lines about sleep in some of his plays. Some parts of the brain activate more during sleep Certain genes release only during sleep. During sleep, the brain neurons activate in some of the most critical parts. Sleep enhances memory and can assist in thinking of solutions to problems. 31% Of drivers have ‘microsleeps’, and will fall asleep at the wheel at least once in their life. Sleeping 5 hours or less consistently can lead to a 50% chance of gaining excess weight. Sleep-deprived individuals tend to be more stressed. Stress lowers the body's natural immunity.

Article 1 Notes (https://www.hopkinsmedicine.org/health/wellness-and-prevention/teenagers-and-sleep-how-much-sleep-is-enough)

Teens need 9 to 9½ hours of sleep per night Due to a second stage of growth Healthy habits such as eating a good breakfast can help regulate healthy sleep. Bad sleep can produce bad habits or effects. Sleep can effect more than energy.

Article 2 Notes (https://www.apa.org/topics/sleep/why)

Millions of people do not get enough sleep 60% Of adults have trouble sleeping 69% Of children have sleeping problems one or more times a week Emotional disorders, obesity, insomnia, and substance abuse can lead to sleep disorders Less stress can lead to better sleep

Article 3 Notes (https://archive.nytimes.com/well.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/03/18/lost-sleep-can-lead-to-weight-gain/?ref=health)

Adults that sleep less have a higher risk of becoming overweight Staying up late and not getting enough sleep increases metabolism Sleep-deprived individuals tend to have a smaller breakfast and a large dinner Lack of sleep can age fat cells ~20 years over time Older fat cells respond less to insulin, which can lead to Diabetes
